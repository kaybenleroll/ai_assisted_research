SILO SYSTEM: GAME MASTER’S SECRETS & MACRO-LORE

RESTRICTED ACCESS // FOR GAME MASTER EYES ONLY

While the Operational & Narrative Primer details the Silo as the players and citizens understand it, this document contains the absolute truth of the world based on the “Operation Fifty” initiative.

Crucially, these secrets should not be the focus of the early or mid-game. A compelling Silo campaign is rooted in the claustrophobia, politics, and daily survival of the citizens. The overarching mysteries outlined below are designed to be the ultimate, late-game paradigm shift.

PART I: THE TRUE REALITY OF THE WORLD

1.1 Operation Fifty

The characters are not in the only Silo. They are in one of fifty identical Silos arranged in a massive subterranean grid beneath the ruins of Atlanta, Georgia.

The Architects

In the mid-21st century, a faction of scientists, intelligence operatives, and industrialists — operating inside the U.S. government but answerable to no elected body — concluded that human civilization was on a trajectory toward self-destruction within three generations. Climate collapse, resource wars, and the proliferation of engineered biological weapons made this, in their modeling, a near-certainty. They called themselves the Pact.

The Pact did not build the Silos as a response to disaster. They built the Silos, then caused one.

Their logic was cold: an organic collapse — war, famine, ecological breakdown — would be chaotic, unpredictable, and likely unsurvivable even underground. So they engineered a controlled extinction event. One they could time, contain (partially), and recover from on a defined schedule.

The Event (Year Zero)

Approximately 180 years before the campaign’s present day, the Pact released a dual-vector nanobot agent into the upper atmosphere via a network of high-altitude dispersal drones. The agent had two components:

  • The Corrosive Seed (“The Bad Seed”): A self-replicating nanobot strain designed to denature organic tissue on direct contact and to aggressively degrade most polymer and rubber compounds — specifically targeting modern sealing technology, engine gaskets, and water filtration membranes. Effectively: anyone caught outside without pre-Pact protective materials dies within minutes. Any vehicle or machine built after a certain year fails within weeks.
  • The Atmospheric Lock: The nanobots bond to particulate matter in the lower atmosphere and persist indefinitely, kept “alive” by solar radiation. They do not degrade. They are not a lingering gas — they are a permanent, invisible, self-sustaining swarm. The sky is lethal, and will remain so until an atmospheric purge technology the Pact buried in the Seed Bunker is activated.

Was It Localised?

No. The catastrophe was global. This is one of the most consequential lies embedded in The Order.

Citizens of the Silo are told the “outside” is a ruined wasteland caused by an unspecified catastrophe, but the implication—carefully managed—is that the damage is regional. This is false. The Pact released the agent at global dispersal altitude. Within eighteen months of Year Zero, the atmospheric nanobot concentration was lethal at every point on Earth’s surface.

The Pact’s own internal records — locked in Silo 1 — estimate that approximately 93% of the global human population died within four years of dispersal. The remaining 7% survived in pre-existing deep shelters, submarines, and isolated subterranean communities with no knowledge of the Silos. What became of them is unknown. The Pact lost contact tracking capability after Year Zero +60. Some may still exist. Some may have found each other. This is a deliberate GM uncertainty — the existence of other survivor populations is a thread the campaign can pull or leave sealed.

The nanobot dome that players sometimes hear referenced is not the global atmosphere. It is a secondary, denser local barrier specifically concentrated over the Atlanta grid — a deliberate kill-layer deployed to prevent any surface survivor from stumbling into the Silo entrances and contaminating the experiment.

The 500-Year Plan (The Full Mechanism)

The plan is not simply “wait underground.” It is a staged, multi-century program to re-seed civilization from scratch, with the Pact’s ideology baked into its foundations. The key phases:

  • Years 0–50 (Stabilisation): The 49 populated Silos are sealed. Internal governance structures (seeded by Pact operatives placed among the initial population) organise along the lines of the Order document. Silo 1 monitors population stability, resource consumption, and rebellion indicators. Any Silo showing early systemic failure is “culled” — Silo 1 terminates its power and water remotely.
  • Years 50–450 (The Pressure Cycle): The Silos are deliberately under-resourced. Births are controlled. Rations are calculated to keep populations just above starvation, maintaining a permanent low-level desperation that suppresses long-term rebellious thinking. Silo 1’s models show that comfortable populations philosophize and revolt. Hungry ones focus on survival. The scarcity is engineered.
  • Year 450 (The Selection): By Year 450, Silo 1 will evaluate which of the remaining Silos is the most psychologically and genetically stable — the best-functioning civilization in miniature. This Silo is designated the Seed. The other surviving Silos are quietly terminated.
  • Year 500 (The Emergence): The Pact’s atmospheric purge device — housed in a bunker connected to the Seed Silo via The Digger — is activated. The purge takes approximately 18 months to render the surface survivable. The Seed Silo’s population emerges, guided by a curated library of pre-Pact knowledge (agriculture, medicine, engineering) that has been held in Silo 1’s archives. The Pact’s descendants, awoken from cryo, take up administrative roles in the new society.

The current year of the campaign is approximately Year 182. The players are living in the Pressure Cycle. The selection has not yet happened. Every Silo is still technically viable.

Silo 1 (The Control Silo)

Silo 1 does not contain a civilian populace. It contains cryo-pods. The architects of the Silos — and their trained successors — work in “shifts,” waking for a few decades to monitor the other 49 Silos before returning to cryo-sleep. Institutionally, they refer to themselves simply as the Order and to their function as Oversight.

  • Silo 1 has direct kill-switch authority over every other Silo: power, water, and atmospheric processors can all be terminated remotely.
  • Silo 1 does not know everything. The long cryo-cycles create institutional memory gaps. Some of the Order’s staff have been awoken to find their predecessor’s notes incomplete, corrupted, or ideologically drifted. The Pact’s original plan has been interpreted and re-interpreted across generations. What the current Order believes the plan is may not be identical to what the original architects intended.

1.2 The Nanobot Dome

The air outside the Silo is not naturally toxic; it is saturated with weaponized, aggressive nanobots (the “bad seed”). * The localized barrier: These nanobots form a dome directly over the 50 Silos, intentionally designed to instantly destroy organic tissue and technology. They were deployed by the Silo creators to ensure no one could leave or cross the wasteland to other Silos. * The Medical “Good Seed”: Conversely, the “miracle” healing in the Silo’s Medical sector is aided by suppressed, beneficial nanobots.

1.3 The Execution Mechanism (The Cleaning)

The Cleaning is a rigged execution designed to ensure compliance. * The Hologram: The visor of the suit projects a pre-recorded, flawless green world. The condemned cleans the camera because they want the people inside to see the “truth.” * The Argon Gas & The Tape: The airlock doesn’t just pump in outside air; it floods the chamber with deadly Argon gas. More importantly, the heat-tape used by IT to seal the suits is intentionally faulty. It degrades within three minutes of exposure to the outside nanobots, ensuring the Cleaner dies precisely at the top of the hill, in full view of the camera, reinforcing the terror of the outside.

PART II: THE LIES WITHIN THE SILO

2.1 The True Purpose of IT

IT is not just the computer department; they are the wardens of the Silo. * “The Order”: The Head of IT and their “Shadow” successor are the only people in the Silo who know about the other 49 Silos. They receive direct orders from Silo 1 via an encrypted terminal hidden in a secret server room. * The Secret Elevator: Unknown to everyone, there is a hidden, single-person elevator shaft running parallel to the IT servers, allowing the Head of IT to travel rapidly between the top levels and the Mids without using the stairs.

2.2 The Reset Button & Amnesia

Uprisings are factored into the Silo’s mathematical models. When a Silo rebels and threatens to breach the doors, Silo 1 orders a “reset.” * The Water Supply: IT introduces a powerful, targeted amnesiac drug into the water supply. * The Purge: The rebellion is violently put down, the survivors are drugged, and history is literally rewritten. This is why there are no records of the “Before” or of past rebellions.

2.3 The Digger

Beneath the Generator on Level 144, behind feet of reinforced concrete and a massive steel vault door, sits The Digger.

The surface is lethal. Any tunnel to another Silo would require boring through hundreds of metres of earth — well below the nanobot-saturated atmosphere. The Pact’s solution was to connect all 50 Silos via a deep subterranean tunnel network, completely bypassing the surface entirely. The Digger is the machine that builds those tunnels.

Its intended function (at Year 500): When Silo 1 activates the emergence protocol, it remotely triggers each Silo’s Digger simultaneously. The machines bore outward and downward along pre-programmed routes until they intersect a central hub bunker — the Seed Vault — located roughly equidistant beneath all 50 Silos. The Seed Vault contains the atmospheric purge device, the pre-Pact knowledge libraries, and the cryo-chambers of the Pact’s final-generation successors. The Seed Silo’s population walks out through the tunnel, activates the purge, and begins repopulating the surface. The other 49 Silos’ tunnels are sealed behind them.

Why each Silo has its own Digger rather than one central machine: The Pact did not trust a single point of failure. If any one tunnel collapses or a Silo’s Digger malfunctions, the others still reach the Vault. It is redundancy engineering.

What this means for players: The Digger was designed to bore toward the Seed Vault — but the boring direction is controlled by a guidance system locked behind the vault door. Nothing physically prevents it from being reprogrammed to bore sideways instead. A tunnel bored horizontally at depth, below the nanobot layer, could reach a neighbouring Silo in weeks. This is the most dangerous fact in the Silo. Silo 1 knows about this possibility and it is the primary reason Shift-Boss Rourke — Silo 1’s covert asset in the Down Deep — has spent years ensuring no one credible ever gets near the vault door.

PART III: CAMPAIGN PACING (“LIVING IN THE SILO”)

The grand lore is fascinating, but if introduced too early, it destroys the intimate, claustrophobic tension of the setting. The players must care about the Silo as their entire universe before they can care about the universe outside it.

Phase 1: The Local Radius (Levels 1-20 Scope)

Focus: Daily Grinds, Scarcity, and Local Politics * Do not mention outside, IT conspiracies, or relics. * The players should be hyper-focused on their jobs. The drama comes from a broken water filter, a stolen chit for extra rations, or a violent dispute between two families in the Hydroponics sector. * Tension: The players are struggling to survive the crushing bureaucracy and brutal labor of their daily lives. The Sheriff is a looming, terrifying threat for minor infractions.

Phase 2: The Vertical Climb (Cross-Sector Scope)

Focus: Inter-departmental Cold Wars and “Glitches” * The players are forced to travel outside their comfort zone (e.g., Porters, investigating a cross-level crime, or trading black market goods). * They get caught in the political crossfire between Mechanical and IT, or the Mayor and Judicial. * The Hook: Introduce tiny, seemingly mundane anomalies. A mechanic notices that Supply provides two different types of heat-tape, but they are forbidden from using the “good” tape on the Cleaning suits. A doctor realizes a patient’s memory of a traumatic event is completely gone after drinking water from a specific reservoir.

Phase 3: The Veil Thins (High-Level Scope)

Focus: The Shadow, Relics, and True Danger * The players find something they shouldn’t. An ancient hard drive (a relic), a blueprint showing a space below Level 144, or they stumble upon the entrance to IT’s secret elevator. * Judicial and the Friends of the Silo actively hunt them. The campaign shifts from a social/survival game to a paranoid espionage thriller. * The Hook: They intercept a garbled radio transmission that isn’t originating from their Silo.

Phase 4: The Paradigm Shift (Endgame)

Focus: Breaking the Silo * This is the climax of the campaign. The players uncover the truth of Operation Fifty. * They might gain access to the Head of IT’s terminal, discover the Digger beneath the oil, or synthesize good heat-tape to survive a Cleaning and walk over the hill to another Silo. * The Hook: They are no longer fighting for their lives; they are fighting to overthrow the 500-year plan.
